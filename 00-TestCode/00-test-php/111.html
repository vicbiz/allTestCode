Dear Jae: Thank you for being a loyal and trusted employee. As you know, the ongoing COVID-19 emergency has had a negative impact on business for Forbes Travel Guide (the “Company”). These conditions require the Company to take a number of difficult, but hopefully temporary, measures. This letter is to inform you that, due to the COVID-19 emergency and related business interruptions, you are being temporarily furloughed without pay from the close of business on Friday, September 18, 2020 to the open of business on Monday, January 4, 2021. Although we anticipate we will reinstate you to work beginning on Monday, January 4, 2021, during and after the furlough period, the Company will evaluate whether the furlough will end or continue. We will then notify you regarding next steps. Please do not perform any work or return to work during the furlough period until directed to do so by the Company.
1. What is a temporary furlough? This temporary furlough is a Company-initiated leave of absence due to the lack of business and government shutdowns related to the novel coronavirus. This action is an operational necessity; it is not a reflection on your work performance. During the furlough, you are still a Company employee unless told otherwise by the Company. You will use any available accrued paid time off prior to taking unpaid leave. After PTO is exhausted, your leave of absence will be unpaid, except to the extent that government benefits apply.
2. Do I remain on group benefits? While furloughed (i.e., on a leave of absence), you are permitted to remain on group benefits so long as you are currently on benefits and remain eligible under the applicable Plan documents. During the furlough period, the Company will pay the employee-paid portion of the group benefits premiums and will continue to pay the employer portion of your benefits premiums while the furlough continues.
3. Am I entitled to Unemployment Insurance Benefits? During the furlough period, you may file for Unemployment Insurance Benefits from the State subject to state-law requirements. Unemployment Insurance Benefits are intended to provide temporary financial assistance to individuals who are not working through no fault of their own. However, you may not be entitled to Unemployment Insurance Benefits if you are using PTO. Please visit the Unemployment Insurance Benefits website for your State for the most up-to-date information regarding:
(1) eligibility,
(2) how to apply,
(3) when and how benefits will be made available during the COVID-19 emergency, and
(4) the weekly dollar amount you may be eligible to receive. The decision to grant or deny a petition for Unemployment Insurance Benefits is made by the State, not the Company.

The website for your State is: https://dol.georgia.gov/ . In addition, the government may provide other benefits (such as paid family leave or temporary disability benefits) to workers who are temporarily unable to work for other reasons, such as parental leave, government-required quarantine, a serious medical condition, or the need to care for a seriously ill family member. Please refer to official government websites for information regarding all potential government-sponsored benefits that may be available to you.

4. How long will the furlough period last? As stated above, the furlough period will be to the open of business on Monday, January 4, 2021. Although we anticipate we will reinstate you to work beginning on Monday, January 4, 2021, during and after the furlough period, the Company will evaluate whether the furlough will end or continue. We will then notify you regarding next steps.

We will communicate updates to you primarily via text message and personal email. Please check your personal email daily and ensure the Company has your most updated contact information.

Upon communication to you that the furlough is ending, you will be expected to promptly return to work as scheduled by the Company. Please note that your employment remains at-will. This is a rapidly changing situation and the furlough period stated in this letter may be changed or terminated at the sole discretion of the Company. This letter does not create an employment contract.

We understand and appreciate the difficulty and anxiety this situation creates, and we anticipate returning you to work as soon as possible. Should you have any questions regarding this matter, please do not hesitate to contact your manager. Thank you for your continued service to the Company.
